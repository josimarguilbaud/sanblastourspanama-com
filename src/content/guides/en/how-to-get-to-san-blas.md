There's no highway straight to San Blas — getting there is part of the adventure, and it's worth knowing what to expect before you go.

## The route, step by step

1. **Pickup in Panama City** — tours include door-to-door pickup from your accommodation, typically between 5:00 and 5:45 AM. Common pickup zones include Obarrio, San Francisco, Amador, Costa del Este, Casco Viejo, Albrook, El Cangrejo, and Punta Paitilla.
2. **4x4 transfer to Carti** — around 2.5 hours by 4x4 vehicle, on a winding mountain road through the Guna Yala comarca (autonomous Guna territory). If you're prone to motion sickness, this is the leg to prepare for.
3. **Boat crossing** — from the Carti embarkation point, a speedboat takes you out to the islands. Total door-to-door time for a day tour runs about 14 hours.

## Practical tips

- **Be ready early.** Passengers should be ready from 5:00 AM (4:30 AM for the Dutch Cays / Cayos Holandeses tour, since it's a longer route).
- **Bring your passport or ID.** It's required — not optional — for the Guna territory entry checkpoint.
- **Cash only past this point.** There are no ATMs anywhere in Guna Yala.
- **Playa Bonita pickups** (Westin, Dreams Playa Bonita) carry a small additional per-person charge due to the extra distance.
- **Pickup location changes** must be made at least 24 hours in advance — same-morning changes aren't possible.

## Can I drive myself?

Technically the road is passable, but almost no visitors do this — the logistics of Guna territory permits, boat coordination, and parking at Carti make an organized tour dramatically simpler, and it's priced to include all of it.
