The single biggest mistake families make booking San Blas on their own is picking the tour with the most islands per day. More stops means more open-water boat time, which is the part that wears kids out and worries parents — not the islands themselves.

## How it's built

We start from islands like Isla Aguja and Perro Grande, both known for shallow, calmer water close to the embarkation point, and build the days around shorter transfers rather than a maximum-coverage route. A private family-size cabin (not a shared dorm) is the base for the overnight portion, and the schedule stays flexible — if a toddler needs a slow morning, the itinerary bends around that instead of the other way around.

## What we need from you

Ages of the kids, how comfortable everyone is on a small boat, and how many nights you want — from there we build a real 3-day/2-night plan and confirm island and cabin availability directly over WhatsApp.
