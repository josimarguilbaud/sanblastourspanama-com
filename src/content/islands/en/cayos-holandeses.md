Ask any San Blas guide which reef has the best snorkeling, and Cayos Holandeses comes up first. It's the farthest point most tours reach — a turquoise lagoon protected by a ring of coral, deliberately positioned away from the busier, closer-in cays.

## Why it's the premium reef

Distance is exactly what protects it — fewer boats make the longer crossing, which means less traffic on the reef and noticeably better-preserved coral than closer, more heavily visited spots. It's the tour to choose specifically if snorkel quality, not island scenery, is your priority.

## What's different about booking this tour

Because of the extra distance, the Dutch Cays tour runs on its own schedule:

- **Earlier departure** — pickup starts around 4:30 AM, earlier than the standard 5:00–5:45 AM for other day tours.
- **Minimum group size applies** — the tour requires a minimum number of travelers per booking, due to the longer boat trip.
- **No child discount** — unlike most other San Blas day tours, adults and children pay the same rate on this one.

## Good to know

- **Best for:** snorkelers who specifically want the best reef quality in the archipelago, not just a general island day.
- **Facilities:** minimal — this is a reef and lagoon stop, not an island with amenities.
- **Compare with:** if a longer boat ride and minimum group size don't fit your plans, the [VIP day tour](/tours) still includes solid guided snorkeling closer to shore.
