As the name suggests ("big dog" in Spanish, part of the Isla Perro group), Perro Grande is noticeably more spacious than many of San Blas's tiny cays — enough room for genuinely wide stretches of white sand instead of a narrow strip you have to share with everyone else on the boat.

## What to expect

The water here is calm and consistently turquoise, well-suited for relaxed swimming rather than snorkeling drama — think floating and unwinding more than chasing fish. The extra space also makes it a comfortable option for overnight stays, with simple beachfront cabins available for travelers doing a multi-day San Blas package.

## Good to know

- **Best for:** travelers who want room to spread out, easy swimming, overnight stays.
- **Facilities:** basic beachfront cabins for overnight guests; shade shelters for day visitors.
- **Route note:** as with all San Blas islands, the exact stops on any given tour depend on the day's conditions and availability.
