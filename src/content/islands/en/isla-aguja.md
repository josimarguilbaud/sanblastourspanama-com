If you're short on boat-transfer time or traveling with young kids, Isla Aguja is often the first stop on the itinerary — it's one of the closest cays to the Carti embarkation point, which means less time bouncing across open water and more time on the beach.

## Why it works for families

The water around Isla Aguja is notably calm and shallow close to shore, without the stronger currents you'll find further out in the archipelago. That, combined with fine white sand and a short boat ride, makes it a favorite first stop for tours carrying families with small children or travelers who are less confident in open water.

## Good to know

- **Best for:** families, short transfer times, easing into the day before more adventurous stops.
- **Facilities:** basic shade shelters; no permanent settlement on the island.
- **Typical itinerary role:** often the first or last stop on a multi-island day tour, given its proximity to the mainland embarkation point.
