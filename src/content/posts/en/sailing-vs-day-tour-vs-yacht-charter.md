**San Blas can be experienced three genuinely different ways: a multi-day sailing crossing between Cartagena and Panama (a border crossing that passes through San Blas waters), a standard day tour or overnight cabin stay departing from Panama City (the most direct way to actually spend time on the islands), or a fully private luxury yacht charter (a bespoke, multi-day experience with no fixed group or schedule).**

## The sailing crossing: Cartagena to Panama

Multi-day sailboat trips between Cartagena, Colombia and Panama typically take 4-5 days and pass through part of the San Blas archipelago on the way to the mainland. These are run by independent sailing operators, not built as dedicated San Blas tours — the boat's real priority is the border crossing itself, and you'll see a portion of San Blas rather than the full range of islands a dedicated tour covers. See our [full breakdown of this route](/guides/sailing-cartagena-to-san-blas) if you're weighing it against flying into Panama directly.

## The standard day tour or overnight stay

This is the most direct way to actually spend real time in San Blas: a fixed door-to-door transfer from Panama City (4x4 plus boat), a genuine itinerary built around the islands and reefs rather than a border crossing, and a fixed, predictable schedule. It's the option most travelers should default to if their goal is specifically "experience San Blas," not "get from Colombia to Panama by boat." See our [full tours page](/tours) for the day-tour and overnight-stay options.

## The private luxury yacht charter

For travelers who want San Blas without sharing a boat, a schedule, or a beach with anyone else, a [private crewed yacht or catamaran charter](/yachts) is a third, distinctly different option: a fully private vessel, your own multi-day itinerary, and access to the archipelago's more remote corners that a standard day tour never reaches. Unlike the sailing crossing, it's not a border crossing with San Blas as a stop along the way — it's built entirely around exploring San Blas itself, just with total privacy and flexibility instead of a shared or fixed schedule.

## How to actually decide

- **Want to cross between Colombia and Panama and see some of San Blas along the way?** The multi-day sailing crossing fits that specific goal.
- **Want the most direct, efficient way to actually experience San Blas's islands, reefs, and culture?** A standard day tour or overnight stay is the right call for most travelers.
- **Want San Blas entirely on your own terms, with no shared group and no fixed schedule, and budget isn't the limiting factor?** A [private yacht charter](/yachts) is built exactly for that.
