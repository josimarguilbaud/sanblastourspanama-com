**No — standard San Blas day tours and overnight stays don't include scuba diving with tanks. San Blas is built around shallow, clear-water snorkeling instead: the reefs here are typically just a few meters deep, ideal for snorkeling but not set up for tank-based dive excursions.**

## Why San Blas isn't a scuba diving destination

San Blas's appeal is specifically shallow water — reefs and wrecks close enough to the surface that you can see them clearly without diving gear. The [shipwreck at Perro Chico](/islands/perro-chico), for example, sits in water shallow enough for complete snorkeling beginners. That same shallowness is exactly what makes it a poor fit for scuba: there's no deep-reef or wreck-diving infrastructure here, because the region's best features aren't built around depth.

There's no dive shop network, no tank rental infrastructure, and no certified dive operators built into the standard tour system the way you'd find in a dedicated diving destination.

## What you get instead

- **The Perro Chico shipwreck** — a sunken vessel now covered in coral, snorkelable by any skill level.
- **The Natural Pool** — a sandbar where you wade waist-deep, known for visible starfish.
- **Dutch Cays (Cayos Holandeses)** — San Blas's most remote and best-preserved reef, included on select premium tours for travelers who specifically want the best reef quality in the archipelago.
