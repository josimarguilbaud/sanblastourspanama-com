**No, San Blas is not the Darién Gap. Both are remote regions of Panama near the Colombian border, but San Blas (Guna Yala) is a Caribbean-coast island archipelago that's a well-established, safe tourism destination, while the Darién Gap is an inland jungle region further south, known for its roadless terrain and migration routes — not a tourist destination.**

## Why people mix them up

The confusion is understandable: both regions sit in eastern Panama, both border Colombia, and both get mentioned in the context of "remote" or "off the beaten path" travel. News coverage of the Darién Gap — usually about the migration route through its dense jungle — sometimes gets conflated in people's minds with any remote part of Panama near Colombia, including San Blas.

## What actually separates them

- **Geography:** San Blas is a Caribbean-coast archipelago of islands. The Darién Gap is an inland, roadless jungle region along Panama's border with Colombia, well south of San Blas's coastal islands.
- **What each is used for:** San Blas is an established, organized tourism destination with daily tours running from Panama City. The Darién Gap has no tourism infrastructure and is primarily known for the migration route that passes through it.
- **How you'd get there:** San Blas tours run a fixed, well-traveled 4x4-and-boat route through Guna Yala. There's no equivalent organized tourist route through the Darién Gap.

## Is San Blas itself safe?

Yes — see our full, honest [safety breakdown for San Blas](/blog/is-san-blas-safe). San Blas has an established, low-risk tourism industry with mandatory life jackets, experienced local Guna guides, and thousands of visitors moving through it regularly. It shares a coastline region with Panama's eastern provinces, but it is not the Darién Gap, and visiting it does not involve the jungle route associated with that name.

## The bottom line

If you've read about the Darién Gap and are now second-guessing a San Blas trip, you can set that concern aside — they're genuinely different places, reached in genuinely different ways, with genuinely different purposes. San Blas is a real, organized, well-run island destination.
