**Yes — you need cash in San Blas. There are no ATMs anywhere in Guna Yala, and cards aren't accepted on the islands themselves. The main cash expense is the community entry tax: $22 for foreign visitors and $7 for residents, paid on arrival at the Guna Yala border.**

## Why cash is unavoidable here

Guna Yala is a self-governed territory with minimal commercial infrastructure by design — see our [eco-tourism guide](/guides/eco-tourism) for why that's a deliberate choice, not an oversight. There's no banking network built into the islands, which means no ATMs and no card terminals once you're past the mainland.

## What you'll actually need cash for

- **The comarca entry tax** — $22 foreigners / $7 residents (a valid passport or residency card is required for the resident rate). This is separate from your tour price and paid in cash at the border.
- **Souvenirs and molas** — buying directly from Guna artisans is cash-only.
- **Extra drinks or snacks** on the islands, beyond what's included in your tour.
- **Any last-minute extras** that come up during your trip.

## How much should you actually bring?

Beyond the entry tax itself, bring more USD than feels strictly necessary — most visitors underestimate this. Between the tax, a mola or two, and incidental extras, planning for roughly the entry tax amount plus $30-50 in additional cash covers most trips comfortably, though this varies by how much you plan to buy.

## What about paying for your tour itself?

Booking and paying for your San Blas tour or stay happens directly with a real person over WhatsApp before you travel — see how that works on our [tours page](/tours). The cash you carry on the day is specifically for the community tax and any on-island extras, not the core tour cost.
