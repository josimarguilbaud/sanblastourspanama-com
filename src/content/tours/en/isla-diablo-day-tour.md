Same starting point as our other day tours, genuinely different destination. The Isla Diablo Tour runs its own route through the Isla Perro group, built around [Isla Diablo](/islands/isla-diablo) itself — relaxed, family-friendly, and distinct enough that it's worth comparing carefully against our VIP tour before you choose.

## The actual hour-by-hour itinerary

We publish this in full because "which islands will I see" shouldn't be a mystery until you're on the boat:

- **8:30 AM** — Arrival at Isla Diablo, free time to swim
- **10:00 AM** — Stop at a neighboring cay within the Isla Perro group
- **11:00 AM** — Guna community visit — a good opportunity to see molas made and sold directly by local artisans
- **12:30 PM** — Lunch
- **1:30 PM** — The Starfish Pool, a separate sandbar stop from the main Natural Pool
- **3:00 PM** — Departure back toward Panama City

## What's included

- ✅ Door-to-door 4x4 transport and boat transfers
- ✅ Two island/cay stops plus the Starfish Pool
- ✅ Guna community visit
- ✅ Lunch with a drink
- ⛔ Guided reef snorkeling (that's specific to our [VIP tour](/tours/vip-day-tour))
- ⛔ Guna Yala community entry tax (paid separately, in cash)

## How it compares to VIP

This is a real fork in the road, not a pricing tier: the VIP tour is built around the Perro Chico shipwreck snorkel, while Isla Diablo's route centers on the Starfish Pool and a longer, more relaxed community visit. If snorkeling is your top priority, go VIP. If you'd rather have more unhurried time at the Guna community stop and don't need guided snorkeling, Isla Diablo is the better fit.
